Gameplay

  1. Each player controls two pieces. The goal is to be the first player to reach the final square (100) with both of your pieces.

  2. Players take turns rolling the dice. After rolling, you'll choose which one of your two pieces to move. The number on the dice indicates how many spaces the selected piece will move.

  3. Entanglement: Players can use an Entanglement Token to entangle their two pieces. Entanglement lasts for a maximum of 5 dice rolls.

    • Activating Entanglement: A player can activate entanglement by using an Entanglement Token. Each player starts with 1 token. Players can use their tokens at any time before rolling the dice, choosing which piece to start the entanglement with. The token is then removed until the next turn.
    • Mirrored Movement: While entangled, the movement of one piece is mirrored by the other. If a player moves one piece forward two spaces, the other piece will also attempt to move forward two spaces.
    • Blocked Movement: If one entangled piece is blocked from moving (e.g., by the edge of the board or another piece), only the unblocked piece will move.
    • Ladders and Snakes: If an entangled piece lands on a ladder or a snake, both pieces will be affected (both will teleport via the ladder or both will slide down the snake).

  4. Breaking Entanglement: Entanglement breaks under the following conditions:

    • One of your pieces lands on a Snake. This also causes the piece to slide down to the bottom of the snake.
    • The entanglement time limit expires. Entanglement lasts for a maximum of 5 dice rolls. The aura around your pieces will blink during the last 2 dice rolls, indicating the time limit is approaching.

  5. Ladders: When a piece lands at the base of a Ladder , it immediately climbs to the top of the ladder.

  6. Snakes: When a piece lands on the head of a Snake, it slides down to the bottom of the snake.

  7. Winning: The first player to reach the final square (100) with both of their pieces wins the game.
