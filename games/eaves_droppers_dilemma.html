Welcome to the Game!

This is a fun guessing game where you play as a spy! Your mission is to intercept a secret quantum code.

The twist is based on a real quantum physics rule: the No-Cloning Theorem. It means you can't copy the quantum messages. Instead, you have to guess how to read each one. A wrong guess can change the message, leaving behind clues that you were there!

Just relax, have fun, and make your best guess for each of the 8 rounds. At the end, we'll see if you got away with it or if your "footprints" gave you away!

What Happened & What We Learned

Any errors in the final key (shown below) occurred when your measurement basis didn't match Alice's. By measuring, you were forced to guess, and a wrong guess had a 50% chance of altering the qubit's state permanently. You couldn't make a copy to check later because the No-Cloning Theorem forbids it.

This disturbance is the core of quantum security. The errors you left behind are the "footprints" that allowed Alice and Bob to detect you.
